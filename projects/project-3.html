Project Overview

This portfolio simulation demonstrates my ability to manage quality control and defect resolution during the final stages of a residential apartment fit-out project. The project focuses on inspection, snagging, defect tracking, and rectification prior to practical completion.

Quality Management Defect Control Inspection & Testing Snagging Close-Out

Project Type

Residential Apartment Fit-Out

Role

Construction Project Manager (Portfolio Project)

Duration

16 Weeks

Project Value

£180,000

Status

Completed (Portfolio Simulation)

Focus

Inspection • Snagging • Rectification • Quality Close-Out

Project Objectives

  • Establish quality inspection procedures during fit-out works
  • Identify defects before practical completion
  • Record and track snagging items in a structured manner
  • Coordinate rectification works with responsible contractors
  • Ensure finished works meet quality standards before handover

Scope of Works

  • Inspection of internal fit-out works
  • Review of partitions and ceilings
  • Inspection of flooring, joinery, and decoration
  • Snagging of electrical and plumbing finishes
  • Defect recording and rectification monitoring
  • Final quality verification prior to practical completion

Key Deliverables

Inspection & Test Plan (ITP)

The Inspection & Test Plan was prepared to define key inspection stages, responsible parties, and acceptance criteria for internal fit-out works.

  • Inspection stages defined for key internal works
  • Acceptance criteria linked to quality standards
  • Responsibilities assigned for inspection and approval
Inspection & Test Plan (PDF)

Skills demonstrated: quality inspection, acceptance criteria, site quality control

Snagging List

A snagging list was prepared during final inspection to identify visible defects and incomplete items requiring correction before practical completion.

  • Defects identified by location and type
  • Priority levels assigned to snagging items
  • Responsible contractors clearly noted
Snagging List (PDF)

Skills demonstrated: defect identification, inspection reporting, contractor coordination

Defects Register

The defects register tracked identified defects through to resolution, including responsible contractor, target rectification date, and verification status.

  • Defects logged in a controlled register
  • Target fix dates assigned
  • Status monitored until close-out
Defects Register (PDF)

Skills demonstrated: defect tracking, close-out control, quality monitoring

Rectification Plan

A structured rectification plan was prepared to ensure defects were corrected, reinspected, and closed before practical completion.

  • Defect rectification process clearly defined
  • Responsibilities assigned to relevant contractors
  • Reinspection process included before close-out
Rectification Plan (PDF)

Skills demonstrated: corrective action planning, defect resolution, quality close-out

Project Outcomes

  • Quality inspection process established during fit-out works
  • Defects recorded and tracked through a structured register
  • Snagging items prioritised and assigned for correction
  • Rectification process defined prior to practical completion

Lessons Learned

  • Early quality inspections reduce pressure at final handover
  • Snagging should be recorded consistently and by location
  • Defects registers improve accountability and contractor follow-up
  • Quality close-out is just as important as programme completion

Tools & Methods Used

  • Excel / Google Sheets (Snagging List, Defects Register)
  • Word (Inspection & Test Plan, Rectification Plan)
  • Structured document control for quality close-out
  • Inspection-based quality management approach

Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrates practical quality management during the final stages of construction. It shows how I inspect works, identify defects, coordinate corrective actions, and support quality close-out before practical completion and handover.
