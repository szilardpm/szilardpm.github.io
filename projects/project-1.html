This portfolio simulation demonstrates my ability to manage a construction project across the full project lifecycle, from planning and cost control through to handover and defects management.

~£117,000

Status

Completed (Portfolio Simulation)

Planning • Cost Control • H&S • EOT • Close-Out

Project Objectives

  • Plan and sequence construction works effectively
  • Control time, cost, and risk
  • Apply health & safety management in line with site practice
  • Manage delays and contractual implications
  • Deliver a structured handover to the client

Scope of Works

  • Site setup and preliminaries
  • Groundworks and foundations
  • Superstructure and roof construction

  • Mechanical & Electrical installations
  • Internal finishes
  • External works
  • Snagging, handover, and defects period

Key Deliverables

Planning & Programme

Project planning was developed using a structured Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), which was then translated into a 20-week baseline construction programme with logical sequencing and dependencies.

  • Structured Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
  • Baseline construction programme
  • Logical sequencing and dependencies

Skills demonstrated: planning logic, sequencing, dependency management

Construction Programme (Gantt Chart)

A 20-week baseline construction programme developed from the project Work Breakdown Structure (WBS). The programme demonstrates logical sequencing of activities, realistic overlaps between trades, and a defined snagging and handover phase.

  • Logical sequencing from site setup to handover
  • Overlaps between envelope, MEP, and internal finishes
  • Programme aligned with cost plan and interim payments
  • Practical Completion identified at Week 20

Download the construction programme (PDF)

Delay & Extension of Time (EOT)

A supplier delay affecting window delivery was analysed against the baseline programme. An Extension of Time (EOT) was prepared based on critical path impact and incorporated into the revised programme.

  • Identified delay cause (windows)
  • Assessed critical path impact
  • Prepared EOT notice and delay analysis
  • Issued revised programme

Project Outcomes

  • Project completed within 1.1% of original budget
  • Approved Extension of Time managed without dispute
  • Health & safety risks identified and controlled
  • Structured handover and defects process established

Lessons Learned

  • Early investigation of ground conditions reduces cost risk
  • Key material lead times must be actively monitored
  • Cost control requires continuous comparison, not end-stage review
  • Clear documentation simplifies handover and close-out

Tools & Methods Used

  • Excel (WBS, Programme, Cost Plan)
  • Word (RAMS, EOT, Cost & Handover documentation)
  • Structured folder-based project control
  • Industry-standard construction management processes
