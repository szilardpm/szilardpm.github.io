Project Overview

This portfolio simulation demonstrates my ability to manage a construction project across the full project lifecycle.

It covers planning, cost control, health & safety, delay management, and structured project handover.

Planning & Programme

I developed the project plan using a structured Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to define all construction activities.

Based on this, I created a 20-week baseline programme with logical sequencing and task dependencies.

This enabled clear identification of the critical path and effective project control.

Construction Programme (Gantt Chart)

I developed a 20-week baseline construction programme based on the WBS.

Activities were logically sequenced from site setup through to handover.

Trade overlaps were planned to improve efficiency without compromising safety or quality.

The critical path was identified to support monitoring and delay mitigation.

Delay & Extension of Time (EOT)

A supplier delay in window delivery impacted the critical path.

I assessed the delay and issued an Extension of Time (EOT).

The programme was updated and mitigation measures were implemented.

Health & Safety (RAMS)

Health and safety was managed through structured RAMS documentation.

Risk Assessments and Method Statements were implemented for key activities.

Toolbox Talks and near miss reporting supported proactive safety management.

Cost Management

I managed project costs from estimation through to final review.

A cost plan aligned with the programme enabled tracking of planned vs actual costs.

Interim valuations ensured consistent cash flow and financial control.

Handover & Close-Out

The project was closed through a structured handover process.

Practical Completion was achieved following inspections and snagging.

Defects were managed during the Defects Liability Period.

Project Outcomes

  • Delivered within 1.1% of budget
  • EOT managed without dispute
  • Health & safety risks controlled
  • Structured handover completed

Lessons Learned

  • Ground conditions must be assessed early
  • Material lead times must be monitored
  • Continuous cost tracking is essential
  • Clear documentation improves handover

Tools & Methods Used

  • Excel (WBS, Programme, Cost Plan)
  • Word (RAMS, EOT, documentation)
  • Structured document control system
  • Industry-standard PM processes

Why This Project Matters
