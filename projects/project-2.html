Project Objectives

Key Deliverables

Planning & Programme

I developed the project plan using a structured Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), tailored to a refurbishment environment where sequencing and access constraints are critical.

Based on this, I created a 28-week baseline programme with logical sequencing, task dependencies, and phased works to manage strip-out, structural upgrades, MEP installations, and internal finishes.

This approach ensured safe coordination of trades within an existing building and allowed effective control of time, access, and risk throughout the project lifecycle.

  • Structured Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
  • Programme planning table
  • Logical sequencing and dependencies

Skills demonstrated: planning logic, sequencing, dependency management

Construction Programme (Gantt Chart)

I developed a 28-week refurbishment programme based on the WBS, ensuring activities were logically sequenced and delivered in controlled phases within an existing building.

The programme incorporated phased works, allowing strip-out, structural modifications, and MEP installations to be completed safely without disrupting subsequent activities.

Key milestones and the critical path were identified to support progress monitoring, delay detection, and effective coordination of trades in a constrained environment.

  • Logical sequencing from surveys to practical completion
  • Phased progression through strip-out, structural works, MEP, and fit-out
  • Programme aligned with progress tracking and cost control
  • Practical Completion identified at the final phase

Project Control & Progress Tracking

I managed project control by comparing planned programme dates against actual site progress, using a structured planned vs actual tracking system.

This enabled early identification of delays, particularly during strip-out and refurbishment phases where unknown conditions can impact progress.

The approach allowed me to maintain control of programme performance and support timely decision-making during live project conditions.

  • Planned vs actual progress monitoring
  • Delay identification and reporting
  • Programme control during live refurbishment works

Health & Safety (RAMS)

I managed health and safety through structured RAMS documentation and incident reporting, aligned with refurbishment and retrofit site practice.

I prepared Risk Assessments and Method Statements for key activities, including groundwork and strip-out operations, where existing building conditions introduced additional risk.

Near miss reporting supported proactive hazard identification and helped maintain safe delivery throughout the project.

  • Risk Assessments
  • Groundworks Method Statement
  • Strip-Out Method Statement
  • Near Miss reporting

Cost Management

I developed a cost plan aligned with the refurbishment WBS and monitored costs throughout the project lifecycle to maintain control within the £280,000 budget.

Planned vs actual cost tracking was used to identify variances, particularly during strip-out and upgrade phases where unforeseen conditions can impact costs.

This enabled better cost visibility and supported proactive decision-making to maintain financial control.

  • Cost planning and budgeting
  • WBS-aligned cost allocation

Lessons Learned

  • Refurbishment projects require stronger phasing and coordination than new build works
  • Early surveys reduce uncertainty in existing buildings
  • Progress tracking is essential for identifying delays in live project environments
  • Clear RAMS documentation supports safer refurbishment delivery
