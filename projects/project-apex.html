Project Overview

Project Apex is a simulated professional construction and project management portfolio based on the transformation of a 450 sqm vacant retail shell into a modern private medical clinic. The project demonstrates full lifecycle construction management, including initiation, procurement, risk control, scheduling, BIM coordination, construction delivery, handover, and post-project review.

Project Objectives

The project was structured around the delivery of a compliant, operationally ready private healthcare facility within a controlled time, cost, quality, and governance framework.

  • Deliver a 450 sqm medical clinic fit-out within a 24-week baseline programme
  • Maintain cost control within a £650,000 project budget
  • Apply APM lifecycle governance from initiation through to handover
  • Use JCT Design & Build 2024 as the recommended procurement and contractual framework
  • Coordinate BIM, MEP, clinical finishes, commissioning, and asset handover
  • Demonstrate professional project controls through WBS, risk, cost, scheduling, and change management

Stakeholder Strategy

I developed a stakeholder management approach to identify key project participants, assess their power and interest, and define suitable communication strategies. This included the client sponsor, local authority, MEP consultant, adjacent retailers, and the public.

  • Identified high-power and high-interest stakeholders requiring close management
  • Defined weekly reporting and financial update routes for the client
  • Included statutory engagement with planning and building control stakeholders
  • Considered third-party disruption risks in a town-centre environment

Construction Delivery & Change Control

During the simulated construction phase, an unmapped sub-floor wet rot defect was discovered beneath the designated X-ray room slab. This created a potential three-week critical path delay and an £18,500 remediation cost. I managed the issue through formal change control, resequencing, and contingency drawdown.

  • Identified latent defect affecting structural suitability for heavy imaging equipment
  • Assessed time and cost impact against the baseline programme
  • Issued an Early Warning Notice under the JCT framework
  • Resequenced dry-lining and partition works to protect the critical path
  • Approved an £18,500 variation using the contingency budget
  • Reduced potential three-week delay to zero net project drift

Commissioning, Handover & Close-Out

I structured the handover phase around statutory, operational, and client-readiness requirements. The project achieved practical completion on schedule, with the £18,500 sub-floor variation absorbed within the original contingency allowance.

  • Completed operational handover checklist
  • Verified hygienic and M&E systems including medical gas pressure testing and HVAC balancing
  • Compiled Health & Safety File and statutory information
  • Managed snagging and punch list close-out
  • Delivered O&M manuals, as-built drawings, and facilities training
  • Achieved practical completion within the 24-week baseline programme

Lessons Learned

The post-project review identified lessons around agile resequencing, hidden utility information, contingency control, and future survey requirements. The project closed below the £650,000 ceiling, with final capital expenditure reported at £603,500.

  • Agile resequencing protected the critical path during latent defect remediation
  • Future clinic acquisitions should complete GPR surveys before lease finalisation
  • Contingency control protected the original budget ceiling
  • £46,500 was returned to the client’s commercial expansion fund at final accounting
  • Structured lessons learned improved future healthcare rollout planning
